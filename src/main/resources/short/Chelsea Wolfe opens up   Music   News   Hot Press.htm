Chelsea Wolfe opens up

The songs she writes are enigmatic, diverse, and utterly fearless. But who is Chelsea Wolfe? Ed Power delves deeper.

The Hot Press Newsdesk, 07 Nov 2013, 14:00



A genuinely intriguing, multi-talented songwriter, California native Chelsea Wolfe is a fascinating swirl of contradictions. Her extremely successful five year career has covered black metal, bare acoustic and electro, with an eclectic performance at Dublin’s Sugar Club earlier exhibiting impressively seamless switches between rock, Goth and whimsical folk.

Since her viral cover of a song by notorious Norwegian death metal band Burzum (whose sole member was imprisoned for murder and arson), Chelsea has been flattered to be associated with the heavy stuff. "I love the peace amidst the chaos" says the 24 year old, who still battles with stage fright and shyness.

Although she dresses like a death metal roadie, her constant musical reinvention, coupled with a complex relationship with an ever-shifting artistic identity, truly defy labeling of any kind.

Her most ambitious album to date, the brand new Pain is Beauty, describes a mature, sophisticated and accomplished artist. As for the woman herself? Read the full article in the new issue of Hot Press, out now.
